==========================================================================
SUMMARY
==========================================================================

I compute photon deflection and Shapiro delay on the DDD substrate in the static spherical sector. Both observables emerge from a single photon-substrate coupling exponent eta = 2, with the leading post-Newtonian deflection angle matching the GR value 4GM/(c^2 b) to within numerical precision over a range of impact parameters. The result extends the gravitational sector of Papers I-II to electromagnetic test particles.

==========================================================================
WHY THIS MATTERS
==========================================================================

Light deflection and Shapiro delay are two of the four classical tests of GR. Reproducing both from a single ansatz on a discrete substrate, with no fitted parameter, is a strong consistency check.

==========================================================================
NOVELTY
==========================================================================

Photon-substrate coupling ansatz n(r) ~ T^eta with eta = 2. Numerical computation of deflection and delay. Comparison with Eddington's 1919 observation and modern VLBI bounds.
